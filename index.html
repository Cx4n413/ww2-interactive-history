A Date Which Will Live in Infamy

December 7, 1941

The Path to War

For years, the U.S had a policy of isolationism while supporting the Allies from the sidelines. However, rising tensions with Japan over embargoes set the stage for the U.S to jump in.

Dec 7, 1941

Pearl Harbor

In a surprise military strike, the Japanese Air Service attacked the U.S base at Pearl Harbor in Honolulu, Hawaii. The attack killed 2,403 U.S. staff and damaged 19 Navy ships.

Dec 8, 1941

The U.S Declares War

Franklin D. Roosevelt addressed Congress, calling December 7 "a date which will live in infamy." Congress officially declared war on Japan, bringing the U.S. into World War 2.

Dec 11, 1941

The Conflict Expands

Due to their Tripartite Pact with Japan, Germany and Italy declared war on the United States. The U.S. responded by fully engaging in the war across both the European and Pacific.

1942 and Beyond

The War Economy

The U.S. economy transformed. Factories shifted from producing regular goods to building tanks, planes, and ships. Millions of Americans, including women, entered the workforce to support the war effort.

Leaders of World War II

The Men Who Shaped History

The Key Figures

These leaders rose to power during turbulent times, shaping the course of the war through their decisions, ideologies, and speeches.

Franklin D. Roosevelt

Government: Democratic President of the United States

Rise to Power: Elected in 1932 during the Great Depression, served four terms as President.

Roosevelt led the US through the New Deal and into WWII after Pearl Harbor.

Winston Churchill

Government: Conservative Prime Minister of the United Kingdom

Rise to Power: Became PM in 1940 after Chamberlain's resignation during WWII.

Known for his inspiring speeches and leadership against Nazi Germany.

Joseph Stalin

Government: Communist General Secretary of the Soviet Union

Rise to Power: Became leader after Lenin's death in 1924, consolidated power through purges.

Led the USSR through industrialization and WWII against Germany.

Adolf Hitler

Government: Führer of Nazi Germany

Rise to Power: Joined Nazi Party in 1919, became Chancellor in 1933, dictator by 1934.

Initiated WWII by invading Poland, responsible for the Holocaust.

Benito Mussolini

Government: Fascist Duce of Italy

Rise to Power: Founded Fasci Italiani in 1919, became Prime Minister in 1922 via March on Rome.

Ally of Hitler, entered WWII in 1940, deposed in 1943.
