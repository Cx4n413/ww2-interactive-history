A Date Which Will Live in Infamy

December 7, 1941

The Path to War

For years, the U.S had a policy of isolationism while supporting the Allies from the sidelines. However, rising tensions with Japan over embargoes set the stage for the U.S to jump in.

Dec 7, 1941

Pearl Harbor

In a surprise military strike, the Japanese Air Service attacked the U.S base at Pearl Harbor in Honolulu, Hawaii. The attack killed 2,403 U.S. staff and damaged 19 Navy ships.

Dec 8, 1941

The U.S Declares War

Franklin D. Roosevelt addressed Congress, calling December 7 "a date which will live in infamy." Congress officially declared war on Japan, bringing the U.S. into World War 2.

Dec 11, 1941

The Conflict Expands

Due to their Tripartite Pact with Japan, Germany and Italy declared war on the United States. The U.S. responded by fully engaging in the war across both the European and Pacific.

1942 and Beyond

The War Economy

The U.S. economy transformed. Factories shifted from producing regular goods to building tanks, planes, and ships. Millions of Americans, including women, entered the workforce to support the war effort.

Leaders of World War II

The Men Who Shaped History

The Key Figures

These leaders rose to power during turbulent times, shaping the course of the war through their decisions, ideologies, and speeches.

Franklin D. Roosevelt

Government: Democratic President of the United States (1933-1945)

Rise to Power: Elected in 1932 during the Great Depression, implemented the New Deal to combat economic crisis. Won unprecedented four terms as President.

Role in WWII: Initially maintained US neutrality but provided aid to Allies through Lend-Lease. After Pearl Harbor attack, declared war on Japan, then Germany and Italy. Led the US through the war, forming alliances with Churchill and Stalin.

Winston Churchill

Government: Conservative Prime Minister of the United Kingdom (1940-1945, 1951-1955)

Rise to Power: Served in various government positions, became PM after Neville Chamberlain's resignation in 1940 due to failure of appeasement policy.

Role in WWII: Famous for his "We shall fight on the beaches" speech. Led Britain through the Blitz and D-Day invasion. Key figure in Allied strategy, working closely with Roosevelt and Stalin.

Joseph Stalin

Government: General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union (1922-1953)

Rise to Power: Became General Secretary in 1922, outmaneuvered rivals like Trotsky through political purges and the Great Purge of 1936-1938.

Role in WWII: Signed non-aggression pact with Hitler in 1939, but invaded by Germany in 1941. Led the USSR's defense, turning the tide at Stalingrad. Key at Tehran and Yalta conferences, shaping post-war Europe.

Adolf Hitler

Government: Führer of Nazi Germany (1934-1945)

Rise to Power: Joined German Workers' Party in 1919, renamed it Nazi Party. Became Chancellor in 1933 through democratic election, then Reichstag Fire led to Enabling Act making him dictator.

Role in WWII: Initiated war by invading Poland in 1939. Implemented Holocaust, killing 6 million Jews. Committed suicide in 1945 as Berlin fell.

Benito Mussolini

Government: Duce of Fascist Italy (1922-1943)

Rise to Power: Founded Fasci Italiani di Combattimento in 1919. Led March on Rome in 1922, appointed PM by King Victor Emmanuel III.

Role in WWII: Ally of Hitler, invaded Ethiopia and Albania. Entered WWII in 1940 but suffered defeats. Deposed in 1943, executed by partisans in 1945.
